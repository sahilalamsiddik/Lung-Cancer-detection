Understand lung cancer before it understands you.

Pulmo Vision is designed to assist with early suspicion of lung cancer from chest CT/X-ray images. It does not replace a doctor. Every prediction must be reviewed and confirmed by a qualified clinician.

Effects on the body

Impact
  • Persistent cough, chest pain and shortness of breath.
  • Unexplained weight loss and continuous tiredness.
  • Reduced ability to do daily activities or exercise.
  • In advanced stages, spread to brain, bones or liver.

How to reduce risk

Prevention
  • Avoid smoking; seek help to quit if you already smoke.
  • Stay away from second-hand smoke and polluted air.
  • Use masks and proper ventilation in dusty workplaces.
  • Exercise regularly and maintain a balanced diet.
No tobacco Clean air Regular checkups

Common reasons

Causes
  • Cigarette, bidi and hookah smoking.
  • Indoor and outdoor air pollution.
  • Occupational exposure (asbestos, chemicals, dust).
  • Family history and genetic factors in some cases.

The risk may still be present even without smoking, so long-lasting symptoms should always be checked by a doctor.

When should you seek medical advice?

Important
  • Cough lasting more than a few weeks, especially with blood.
  • Breathing difficulty or chest pain that is not improving.
  • Sudden weight loss or loss of appetite without clear reason.
  • Any abnormal lung scan result reported by a radiologist.

Pulmo Vision is a support tool only. Always consult a doctor for a final diagnosis and treatment plan.

How to use Pulmo Vision

Guide
  1. 1
    Go to the Prediction tab from the top navbar.
  2. 2
    Choose the correct modality type (CT, X-ray or other chest image).
  3. 3
    Click on Select image file and upload the scan that you want to analyse.
  4. 4
    Check the preview on the left to confirm that the image looks correct (no rotation or cropping issues).
  5. 5
    Press Predict. The model will process the image and show the predicted probability, stage and notes on the right.
  6. 6
    Share the scan and the model output with a qualified doctor for final interpretation.

Limitations

Read
  • The model can be wrong or uncertain.
  • Low-quality or noisy scans reduce accuracy.
  • Non-cancer lung disease can look similar on images.
  • This tool is not approved as a standalone medical device in most countries.
